# Example Customer Story: Acme Corp AI Deployment

**Title:** Proving AI at Scale: How Acme Corp Achieved 82% Deflection and 4.8/5 CSAT
**Word Count:** ~1,100 words
**Format:** Proof-oriented customer story

---

Most companies deploying AI agents face the same frustration: the technology works in demos but breaks down at scale. Conversations go off-script. Responses sound robotic. Customer satisfaction drops.

But it doesn't have to be that way. At Acme Corp, John Doe proved that AI agents can deliver consistent, high-quality interactions at enterprise scale, if you build the right guardrails.

## The Results

Doe, a Senior Systems Administrator at Acme Corp, used the company's AI platform to build agents that now handle 82% of inbound support inquiries without human intervention. Customer satisfaction scores hit 4.8 out of 5.

"Our lowest week was 78% deflection. Our highest was 86%," Doe says. "That consistency is what convinced leadership this wasn't a fluke."

Instead of treating AI as a ticket deflection tool, Doe redesigned how the entire support intake process works. The AI agent handles identification, categorization, and resolution for routine issues. Complex cases get handed to human agents with full context.

## Building Deterministic Control

### The Rule-Based Approach

Doe isn't a developer by training. He's an admin who learned to build AI workflows through the platform's low-code tools. That background shaped his approach.

"I didn't want the AI making creative decisions," Doe explains. "I wanted it following rules. If a customer asks about billing, the agent follows the billing workflow. Every time."

To prevent hallucination, Doe implemented explicit guardrails: the AI agent can only reference approved knowledge base articles. If a question falls outside the mapped scenarios, the agent transfers to a human immediately rather than guessing.

"The customer never knows the difference between a smooth AI resolution and a smooth handoff. That's the goal."

### Conversation Design at Scale

The second breakthrough was treating conversation flows as modular components. Doe built 47 conversation templates that cover the most common support scenarios. Each template has three parts: identify the customer, resolve the issue, confirm satisfaction.

Using the platform's script editor, Doe configured specific response patterns for each scenario. A billing question gets a different tone than a technical troubleshooting request.

"I treat these templates like building blocks," Doe says. "When a new issue type comes up, I create a new block. It takes me about two hours instead of two weeks."

## Validating the Results

### Proving It Works

To ensure quality, Doe conducted a manual audit of 500 AI-handled conversations over a four-week period. He read each transcript and scored it against three criteria: accuracy, tone, and resolution.

The results were definitive: 96% of conversations met all three criteria. The remaining 4% were edge cases where the AI correctly identified it couldn't help and transferred to a human.

"That 96% number is what I show leadership," Doe says. "It's not a projected metric. I read those conversations myself."

Compared to the 71% quality score from their previous manual triage process, the improvement was dramatic.

## The Full Picture

Since deploying nine months ago, Acme Corp's support transformation has been sustained:

**Deflection Rate:** 82% average (78-86% range weekly)
- Up from 0% (no AI prior to deployment)

**Customer Satisfaction:** 4.8/5 CSAT
- Matching their human-agent benchmark of 4.7/5

**Quality Score:** 96% (from 500-conversation audit)
- Up from 71% with previous manual triage

**Efficiency:** 47 modular conversation templates
- Each built in approximately 2 hours

"We handle 12,000 support interactions per month now," Doe says. "Before AI, we needed 15 agents to cover that volume. Now we have 8 agents focused on complex issues, and customer satisfaction is actually higher."

## What's Next

Two years ago, Doe was manually routing support tickets. As a systems admin, he's seen AI go from experiment to core infrastructure.

"The biggest change isn't the technology," Doe says. "It's that my team went from answering the same questions all day to actually solving problems."

Doe is already expanding the approach to onboarding workflows. He predicts a 60% deflection rate for new customer setup questions within six months.

Doe's success proves that you don't need a team of developers to build effective AI agents. You need clear rules, validated workflows, and the patience to audit your results.

"My advice? Don't try to make the AI smart. Make it predictable. Customers don't want creative answers. They want correct answers, fast."
