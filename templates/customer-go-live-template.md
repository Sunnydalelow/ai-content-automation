## Social Media Post (LinkedIn)

### Structure
1. **Hook** (1-2 sentences): Congratulate customer + what they launched
2. **Context** (2-3 sentences): Why this matters, what problem it solves
3. **Impact** (1-2 sentences): Specific metrics or scale achieved
4. **Close** (1 sentence): Forward-looking statement or trust/scale theme

### Template

```
Congratulations to [Customer] on [specific achievement/go-live]!

[1-2 sentences on what makes this significant - new operating model, scale, complexity, etc.]

[Key metric or impact statement that demonstrates trust/scale/value.]

[Optional: Call out key stakeholders.] [Forward-looking statement.]
```

---

## Example Posts (Reference)

### Example 1: Enterprise, Complex Environment
```
Excited to see Acme Corp bring AI-assisted operations into production.

Not just layering AI onto existing processes, but redesigning the operating
model and embedding it into how teams work.

Delivering real impact inside a complex, highly regulated environment
is no small feat.

Congratulations Jane Smith, the Acme team, and everyone across both
organizations who made it happen. Proud to be building the future together.
```

### Example 2: High Volume, Metrics-Driven
```
Congratulations to the TechStart team on a successful product go-live!

This expands their 10% screening rate to reach 100% of applicants,
many outside business hours. The team now has more time to focus on
connecting with applicants.

97,000 transactions later, this is a practical example of AI earning
trust at scale.
```

## Blog Post (Optional - for major go-lives)

**Structure:**
1. **Lede** (2-3 sentences): The achievement and why it matters
2. **Challenge** (1 paragraph): What problem the customer faced
3. **Solution** (2-3 paragraphs): How they deployed, what they built
4. **Results** (1-2 paragraphs): Metrics, impact, scale
5. **Quote** (1 paragraph): Customer stakeholder quote
6. **Looking Forward** (1 paragraph): What's next, broader implications

**Length:** 400-600 words

## Distribution Checklist

- [ ] Post to LinkedIn (personal or company account)
- [ ] Tag customer company page (if available)
- [ ] Tag key stakeholders (with permission)
- [ ] Use relevant hashtags
- [ ] Share in internal channels
- [ ] Optional: Create visual asset and attach to post
- [ ] Optional: Cross-post to Twitter/X (shorter version)

---

## Notes

- **Speed matters:** Aim to post within 24-48 hours of go-live
- **Customer first:** Always prioritize customer comfort and approval
- **Metrics matter:** Specific numbers make or break these posts
- **Production only:** Save go-live content for real production deployments
