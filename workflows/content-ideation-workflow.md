# Content Ideation Workflow

**Purpose:** Turn market signals into high-performing content

**Process:** Market Listening → Content Ideas → Validation → Production

---

## Overview

This workflow connects market intelligence to content creation, ensuring your content addresses what the market actually cares about.

**Timeline:** Continuous (ideas captured weekly, validated monthly)

---

## Phase 1: Capture Market Signals (Ongoing)

### Step 1: Listen & Capture
**Owner:** PMM (You)
**Frequency:** Daily/Weekly
**Tool:** Market Listening Guide

- [ ] Follow weekly listening routine
- [ ] Capture insights using template
- [ ] Save to `/research/content-ideas/`
- [ ] Tag by urgency (Hot / Warm / Cold)

**Output:** Raw insights captured in research folder

---

## Phase 2: Pattern Recognition (Weekly)

### Step 2: Weekly Synthesis
**Owner:** PMM
**Time:** Friday, 30-60 minutes

Review this week's captured insights:

**Questions to ask:**
1. **What topics appeared multiple times?** (Multiple sources = validated signal)
2. **What surprised you?** (Unexpected trends worth exploring)
3. **What are customers asking about?** (Direct needs)
4. **What are competitors ignoring?** (Opportunity gaps)
5. **What's getting high engagement?** (Proven interest)

**Action:**
- [ ] Group similar insights together
- [ ] Identify 3-5 themes/clusters
- [ ] Rank by priority (see prioritization framework below)

**Output:** Prioritized list of content themes

---

## Phase 3: Content Ideation (Weekly)

### Step 3: Generate Content Ideas
**Owner:** PMM
**Time:** 30 minutes after synthesis

For each priority theme, brainstorm content:

#### Use This Framework:

**Theme:** [Topic name]

**Content Angles:**
1. **How-To / Guide:** "How to [achieve outcome] with [approach]"
2. **Problem-Solution:** "[Problem] won't work, but [solution] can"
3. **List/Tips:** "X ways to [achieve goal]"
4. **Thought Leadership:** "Why [trend] matters for [audience]"
5. **Customer Story:** "How [customer] [achieved result]"
6. **Contrarian:** "Why [common belief] is wrong"
7. **Explainer:** "What is [concept]? A complete guide"
8. **Comparison:** "[A] vs [B]: Which is right for you?"

**Example:**
- **Theme:** "AI agents struggling with reliability"
- **Angles:**
  - How-to: "Guide to Achieving Reliable Agent Behavior"
  - Problem: "Doomprompting Won't Get You Reliable Agents"
  - List: "6 Ways to Ruin a Perfectly Good AI Agent"
  - Explainer: "What Causes AI Agent Hallucinations?"

**Output:** 5-10 specific content ideas per theme

---

## Phase 4: Validation (Before Production)

### Step 4: Validate Content Ideas
**Owner:** PMM + Stakeholders
**Time:** 15-30 minutes per idea

Before producing content, validate the idea:

#### Validation Checklist

**✅ Audience Fit**
- [ ] Is this relevant to our target personas?
- [ ] What specific pain point does it address?
- [ ] Will they care enough to read/share?

**✅ Timing**
- [ ] Is this topic timely? (Hot now vs. evergreen)
- [ ] Are we early, on-time, or late to this conversation?
- [ ] Any external timing factors? (Events, launches)

**✅ Differentiation**
- [ ] What's our unique angle?
- [ ] How is this different from competitor content?
- [ ] What proprietary insights can we add?

**✅ Strategic Alignment**
- [ ] Does it support our positioning (your product vision)?
- [ ] Does it feature our products naturally?
- [ ] Does it advance our thought leadership?

**✅ Search/SEO Potential**
- [ ] Is anyone searching for this?
- [ ] What's the search volume?
- [ ] Can we rank for related keywords?

**✅ Resource Availability**
- [ ] Do we have the expertise internally?
- [ ] Can we produce this in available timeframe?
- [ ] Do we need customer/partner participation?

**Decision:**
- ✅ **Green light** → Add to content calendar
- ⚠️ **Needs work** → Refine angle and re-validate
- ❌ **Park it** → Good idea, wrong time (revisit later)

**Output:** Validated content ideas ready for calendar

---

## Phase 5: Prioritization (Monthly)

### Step 5: Prioritize Content Calendar
**Owner:** PMM + Content Team
**Time:** Monthly planning session (1 hour)

Use this framework to prioritize validated ideas:

### Priority Matrix

#### Tier 1: MUST DO (Produce within 2 weeks)
- 🔥 Hot trending topics (risk of being late)
- 🎯 Direct customer asks (repeated questions)
- ⚡ Competitive response needed
- 📅 Tied to launches/events

#### Tier 2: SHOULD DO (Produce within 1 month)
- 📈 Rising trends (early but not urgent)
- 🎓 Educational content (evergreen value)
- 💪 Thought leadership (strategic positioning)
- 🔍 High SEO potential (long-term traffic)

#### Tier 3: NICE TO HAVE (Produce within quarter)
- 💡 Interesting ideas (low urgency)
- 🔄 Refresh old content (updates)
- 🧪 Experimental angles (test and learn)

#### Tier 4: BACKLOG (Revisit next quarter)
- 📁 Good ideas, wrong time
- 🌱 Emerging topics (too early)
- 🤔 Needs more validation

---

## Phase 6: Production Planning (Monthly)

### Step 6: Add to Content Calendar
**Owner:** PMM
**Time:** After prioritization

For each Tier 1 & 2 idea:

**Define:**
- [ ] **Content type** (Blog, whitepaper, customer story, etc.)
- [ ] **Target length** (Based on examples in `/examples/`)
- [ ] **Target audience** (Developers, executives, etc.)
- [ ] **Key messages** (From messaging framework)
- [ ] **Angle/hook** (What makes it compelling?)
- [ ] **CTA** (What action do we want readers to take?)
- [ ] **Due date** (Based on priority)
- [ ] **Owner** (Who's writing?)
- [ ] **Reviewers** (Who needs to approve?)

**Output:** Fully planned content ready for production

## Monthly Content Planning Meeting Agenda

### Use This Structure

**Duration:** 60-90 minutes
**Attendees:** PMM, Content team, Product marketing lead

#### 1. Review Last Month (15 min)
- [ ] What performed well? (Views, engagement, conversions)
- [ ] What underperformed? (Learn and adjust)
- [ ] Any gaps in coverage?

#### 2. Market Intelligence Review (20 min)
- [ ] Key trends from past month
- [ ] New competitor content to address
- [ ] Customer feedback themes
- [ ] Upcoming events/launches

#### 3. Prioritize New Ideas (20 min)
- [ ] Review validated content ideas
- [ ] Score and prioritize using framework
- [ ] Assign to tiers

#### 4. Build Next Month's Calendar (20 min)
- [ ] Slot Tier 1 content (must produce)
- [ ] Slot Tier 2 content (capacity allowing)
- [ ] Assign owners and dates
- [ ] Identify dependencies

#### 5. Resource Check (10 min)
- [ ] Do we have capacity?
- [ ] Any blockers?
- [ ] Need external support? (Design, legal, customers)

**Output:** Content calendar for next 30 days

## Quick Content Idea Generator

### When You Need Ideas Fast

**Step 1:** Pick a format
- Guide: "Complete guide to [topic]"
- List: "X ways to [achieve goal]"
- Problem: "[Problem] won't work, but [solution] will"
- Explainer: "What is [concept]?"

**Step 2:** Pick a topic from market signals
- Check `/research/content-ideas/` for captured insights
- Review trending hashtags from this week
- Ask: What did customers ask about this week?

**Step 3:** Add your angle
- your company perspective
- Agentic enterprise framing
- Customer success lens
- Technical depth

**Step 4:** Validate quickly
- Does it pass the "So what?" test?
- Is someone actually searching for this?
- Can we produce it in our timeframe?

## Content Types by Market Signal

### Match Signal to Format

| Market Signal | Best Content Type |
|---------------|-------------------|
| **Hot trending topic** | Blog post (quick turnaround) |
| **Repeated customer question** | How-to guide or FAQ |
| **Competitor announcement** | Thought leadership response |
| **New use case emerging** | Customer story |
| **Complex topic confusion** | Whitepaper or comprehensive guide |
| **High search volume** | SEO authority page |
| **Viral social discussion** | Social post or short blog |
| **Analyst report** | Strategic whitepaper |
