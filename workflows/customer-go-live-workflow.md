# Customer Go-Live Workflow

This workflow guides the creation of go-live announcements when customers deploy your product to production.

---

## Overview

**Content Type:** Customer Go-Live Announcement  
**Primary Deliverable:** LinkedIn social media post (4-7 sentences)  
**Optional Deliverables:** Canva asset, blog post  
**Timeline:** 24-48 hours from go-live notification  
**Priority:** High (time-sensitive)

---

## When to Use This Workflow

✅ **Create go-live content when:**
- Customer deploys your product product to production
- Customer has approved public announcement
- You have verified metrics or impact data
- Deployment is significant enough to celebrate

❌ **Skip or delay when:**
- Still in pilot/beta phase
- Customer prefers to stay private
- Metrics are unclear or unverified
- Awaiting customer approval

---

## Workflow Stages

### Stage 1: Notification & Data Gathering
**Duration:** 1-2 hours  
**Owner:** PMM / Content team

**Trigger:**
- FDE notifies of customer go-live
- Customer Success mentions production deployment
- Monitoring Google Sheet shows "Go-live" status

**Tasks:**
1. **Confirm go-live details:**
   - [ ] Customer name
   - [ ] Product/feature deployed (Voice, Script, etc.)
   - [ ] Go-live date
   - [ ] Deployment status (pilot vs. production)

2. **Gather metrics:**
   - [ ] Before/after data
   - [ ] Volume numbers (calls handled, tickets resolved, etc.)
   - [ ] Time savings or efficiency gains
   - [ ] User adoption data

3. **Identify stakeholders:**
   - [ ] Key customer contact(s)
   - [ ] Title(s)
   - [ ] Permission to mention by name

4. **Get approvals:**
   - [ ] Customer comfortable with public announcement?
   - [ ] Timing coordination needed?
   - [ ] Any sensitive information to avoid?

**Output:** Data collection document with all required information

---

### Stage 2: Content Creation
**Duration:** 1-2 hours  
**Owner:** PMM Writer / AI Agent

**Input Required:**
- Data from Stage 1
- Template: [customer-go-live-template.md](../templates/customer-go-live-template.md)
- Agent guide: [customer-go-live-writer.md](../agents/customer-go-live-writer.md)

**Tasks:**
1. **Draft social post:**
   - [ ] Use template structure (Hook → Context → Impact → Close)
   - [ ] Include specific metrics
   - [ ] Name stakeholders (with permission)
   - [ ] Keep to 4-7 sentences
   - [ ] Check tone is celebratory but professional

2. **Create Canva asset (optional):**
   - [ ] Determine if visual will strengthen post
   - [ ] Choose most impressive metric for headline
   - [ ] Use Customer Quote Card template
   - [ ] Include customer + your product logos

3. **Draft blog (optional - for major go-lives):**
   - [ ] Expand social post into 400-600 words
   - [ ] Add implementation details
   - [ ] Include customer quote if available
   - [ ] Follow blog structure from template

**Output:** 
- Draft social post
- Draft Canva asset (if applicable)
- Draft blog (if applicable)

---

### Stage 3: Review & Approval
**Duration:** 2-4 hours (may need customer input)  
**Owner:** PMM Lead / Content Lead

**Tasks:**
1. **Internal review:**
   - [ ] Verify all metrics with FDE
   - [ ] Check against brand guidelines
   - [ ] Confirm stakeholder name permissions
   - [ ] Legal/compliance review if needed

2. **Customer approval (if required):**
   - [ ] Share draft with customer contact
   - [ ] Get explicit approval for metrics
   - [ ] Confirm timing is appropriate
   - [ ] Address any feedback

3. **Final edits:**
   - [ ] Incorporate feedback
   - [ ] Final proofread
   - [ ] Confirm all links work
   - [ ] Check formatting

**Output:** Approved final content ready to publish

---

### Stage 4: Asset Production
**Duration:** 1-2 hours (if Canva asset needed)  
**Owner:** Designer / PMM

**Tasks (if creating Canva asset):**
1. **Create design:**
   - [ ] Open Customer Quote Card template
   - [ ] Add headline (key metric)
   - [ ] Add subhead (customer + product)
   - [ ] Add stat/quote
   - [ ] Add logos (customer + your product)
   - [ ] Apply brand colors

2. **Review:**
   - [ ] Check readability at mobile size
   - [ ] Verify logo quality
   - [ ] Confirm metrics match approved copy

3. **Export:**
   - [ ] Export as high-res JPG
   - [ ] Save source file for future edits
   - [ ] Name file: `[customer]-go-live-card-[YYYY-MM-DD].jpg`

**Output:** Production-ready Canva asset

---

### Stage 5: Publication
**Duration:** 30 minutes  
**Owner:** PMM / Social Media Manager

**Tasks:**
1. **Post to LinkedIn:**
   - [ ] Copy approved text
   - [ ] Attach Canva asset (if created)
   - [ ] Tag customer company page (if available)
   - [ ] Tag stakeholders (with permission)
   - [ ] Add hashtags: #your product #AI #CustomerSuccess
   - [ ] Schedule or post immediately

2. **Publish blog (if applicable):**
   - [ ] Post to your company blog
   - [ ] Add to customer stories collection
   - [ ] Share link in social post comments

3. **Cross-promote:**
   - [ ] Share in internal Slack channels
   - [ ] Add to customer momentum page
   - [ ] Include in weekly newsletter
   - [ ] Optional: Cross-post to Twitter/X

**Output:** Live social post + optional blog

---

### Stage 6: Tracking & Follow-up
**Duration:** Ongoing  
**Owner:** PMM / Content Ops

**Tasks:**
1. **Track performance:**
   - [ ] Monitor engagement (likes, comments, shares)
   - [ ] Track click-through (if blog link included)
   - [ ] Note any customer response/sharing

2. **Update records:**
   - [ ] Add to content tracking system
   - [ ] Update customer record with deliverable
   - [ ] Save final files to appropriate folders

3. **Notify stakeholders:**
   - [ ] Share post link with customer contact
   - [ ] Update FDE/CSM that content is live
   - [ ] Share metrics with leadership (if significant)

**Output:** Performance data, updated records

## Decision Points

### Should we create a blog?

**Yes, if:**
- Major enterprise customer
- Exceptional metrics (>2X improvement, massive scale)
- Interesting implementation story
- Customer has approved quote
- Strategic importance (industry first, new product, partnership)
- Customer wants more visibility

**No (social only) if:**
- Standard go-live
- Limited metrics available
- Quick turnaround needed
- Customer prefers lower profile
- Blog resources constrained

### Should we create a Canva asset?

**Yes, if:**
- Strong visual metric (97,000 interviews, 10% → 100%)
- Want to boost social engagement
- Customer has recognizable brand
- Post will be widely shared
- Time permits (adds 1-2 hours)

**No (text only) if:**
- Tight timeline (< 24 hours)
- Metrics not visually compelling
- Want to keep it simple
- Customer logo not available

## Quality Checklist

Before publishing, verify:

**Content:**
- [ ] Customer is the hero, not the product
- [ ] Includes specific metrics or impact
- [ ] Production deployment emphasized
- [ ] Stakeholders named (when appropriate)
- [ ] Forward-looking close
- [ ] 4-7 sentences for social post
- [ ] No marketing jargon

**Accuracy:**
- [ ] All metrics verified with FDE
- [ ] Customer name spelled correctly
- [ ] Product names follow approved terminology
- [ ] Stakeholder titles accurate
- [ ] Go-live date confirmed

**Approvals:**
- [ ] Customer approved (if required)
- [ ] Legal/compliance cleared (if needed)
- [ ] Stakeholder permission to use names
- [ ] Brand guidelines followed

**Distribution:**
- [ ] Posted to primary channel (LinkedIn)
- [ ] Tagged appropriately
- [ ] Shared in internal channels
- [ ] Tracked in content system

## Common Issues & Solutions

### Issue: "Metrics aren't available yet"
**Solution:** 
- Post initial congratulations without specific metrics
- Follow up with metrics post once available
- Or delay publication until metrics confirmed

### Issue: "Customer wants approval but is slow to respond"
**Solution:**
- Set clear timeline ("need approval by X date")
- Offer to share draft in real-time call
- Have backup customers ready for go-live content
- Post generic congratulations, save detailed post for later

### Issue: "Go-live happened last week, feels stale"
**Solution:**
- Lead with "Last week" or specific date
- Focus on impact/results rather than announcement
- Add "update" framing: "Update on TechStart's Voice deployment..."

### Issue: "Multiple products launched at once"
**Solution:**
- Choose primary/most impressive product to lead with
- Mention others in context: "...adding to their your product deployment"
- Consider separate posts if both are significant

### Issue: "Not sure if this warrants a post"
**Solution:**
- If it's true production deployment with metrics → YES
- If it's pilot or small rollout → Maybe wait
- If customer is P0/P1 → Lean toward yes
- When in doubt, ask: "Would our customers find this inspiring?"

## Timeline Best Practices

**Ideal Timeline:**
- Day 0: Go-live happens
- Day 1: Notification received, data gathered
- Day 1-2: Content created and approved
- Day 2: Published

**Acceptable Timeline:**
- Within 1 week of go-live

**Too Late:**
- More than 2 weeks post-go-live (unless significant update/milestone)

**Speed vs. Quality:**
- Accuracy > Speed (always verify metrics)
- Customer approval > Timeline (respect their timeline)
- Production > Pilot (wait for real deployment)

## Success Metrics

Track these for each go-live post:

**Engagement:**
- LinkedIn impressions
- Likes, comments, shares
- Click-through rate (if blog linked)

**Business Impact:**
- Customer shares/amplifies
- Internal visibility (leadership mentions)
- Follow-on opportunities (media, speaking)

**Process:**
- Time from notification to publication
- Number of revisions needed
- Customer satisfaction with content

**Portfolio:**
- Total go-live posts per quarter
- Ratio of P0/P1 customers covered
- Blog vs. social-only split

## Integration with Content Lifecycle

**How go-live fits into broader content system:**

1. **Discovery:** FDE notifies of go-live (may come from Google Sheet monitoring)
2. **Quick Creation:** Follow this workflow (1-2 days)
3. **Publication:** Post immediately (time-sensitive)
4. **Tracking:** Add to content tracking system
5. **Portfolio:** May lead to deeper customer story later

**Relationship to Customer Stories:**
- Go-live = Quick announcement, metrics-focused, social
- Customer Story = Deep dive, interview-based, blog + assets
- Go-live can be a stepping stone to full customer story

## Notes

- **Speed matters:** Go-lives are time-sensitive, aim for 24-48 hour turnaround
- **Customer first:** Always prioritize customer comfort and approval
- **Metrics matter:** Specific numbers make or break these posts
- **Production only:** Save go-live content for real production deployments
- **Template is a guide:** Adapt structure based on available information
